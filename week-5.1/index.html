# Oct 17 Update ## Calculations I used real world numbers for the maximum magnification of a fresnel lens (~800x) and then took the highest heat achieved, around 1300k. Given a starting area of around a square meter, this means that there were roughly 25 square centimeters that were at this maximum temperature. Since the temperature is inversely propotional to area, I calculated that somewhere between 56 and 113 square centimeters can be heated to the temperature needed to melt our eutectic mixture, for every square meter of collection we have. With the thermal storage container having a length of 15 cm in the shortest direction, it appears that any side can be heated, even if we have 4 square meters of collection. This is because 15 cm by 15 cm grants us an area of 225 square centimeters, and going with a smaller area will ensure that the pellets melt more quickly. We should not heat the top of the container, as this space will likely have air underneath the steel, with the molten mass lying as low as possible.
